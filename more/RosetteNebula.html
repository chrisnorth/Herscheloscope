Rosette Nebula

Information

The Rossette Nebula is a cloud of gas and dust containing enough material to make 10,000 stars like our Sun. This false-colour image shows the temperature of different parts of the nebula. The red blobs at the bottom are protostars. The blue region at the top is being heated by a cluster of hot, young stars. They will live short lives (less than 1/10th as long as our Sun's). By the time these die, the protostars will have evolved into fully-fledged stars in their own right.

Constellation: Monoceros the Unicorn

Distance: 5500 light years
