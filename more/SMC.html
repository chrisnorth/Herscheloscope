Small Magellanic Cloud

Information

The Small Magellanic Cloud is a galaxy that orbits the Milky Way. It's too small to have developed a spiral structure. This false-colour image uses data from Herschel and NASA's Spitzer satellite. Red and green show cloud interstellar dust; the bright, bluer areas are regions of active star formation. The Magellanic Clouds let us study star formation in galaxies other than our own, but still close enough for us to see lots of detail.

Diameter: 7 thousand light years

Mass: 7 billion solar masses

Constellation: Tucana the Toucan

Distance: 200 thousand light years
