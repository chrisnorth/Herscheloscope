The Eagle Nebula

Information

In this false-colour image of the Eagle Nebula, blue shows where stars are being born, heating up the surrounding clouds of gas and dust. The red clouds are interstellar dust, so cold that they’re almost at absolute zero, -273oC. The famous 'Pillars of Creation' photo taken by Hubble was of the Eagle Nebula. The three 'pillars' can be seen in the lower centre of this image, glowing to Herschel’s far-infrared eyes.

Diameter: 14 light years

Constellation: Cygnus the Swan

Distance: 6500 light years
