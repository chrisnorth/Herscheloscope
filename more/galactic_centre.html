A Twisted Ring in the Galactic Centre

Information

The centre of our galaxy contains a ring of gas and dust 600 light-years across, just 15oC above absolute zero, encircling the Milky Way's central supermassive black hole. Herschel has shown us that this ring is twisted, for reasons we don't yet understand. It's visible in this false-colour image as the yellow band snaking across the picture. Warmer dust in the core of the galaxy is seen in blue.

Distance: 30,000 light years

Diameter: 600 light years

Constellation: Sagittarius the Archer
